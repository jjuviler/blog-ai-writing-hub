HubSpot Branding

This skill is shared with all HubSpot blog writers — you should have access automatically. To activate it, go to Customize → Skills in Claude and look for it under Shared Skills. Select the skill, then toggle it on in the top right. If you don't see the skill, reach out to your manager.

Current version: v2

Overview

The HubSpot Branding skill applies the official HubSpot 2025 Brand System consistently to any artifact — HTML pages, dashboards, or Google Slides decks. It knows the full brand system: fonts, colors, logo rules, slide templates, and accessibility pairings, so you don't have to look anything up.

When to Use

  • Creating or styling HTML artifacts, dashboards, or reports with HubSpot branding
  • Building Google Slides presentations that need to follow HubSpot brand guidelines
  • Any time you need to apply HubSpot colors, fonts, or layout specs to a Claude artifact
  • Checking whether a design matches the official 2025 brand system

How to Use

  1. Open a new Claude chat with the HubSpot Branding skill active
  2. Describe the artifact you want to create — a slide deck, dashboard, report, etc.
  3. Claude will apply the full 2025 brand system automatically
  4. Iterate with Claude as needed — adjust layouts, colors, content

Example Prompts

Create a 10-slide Google Slides deck summarizing our Q2 blog performance. Use HubSpot branding.
Build an HTML dashboard for this data with HubSpot branding.
Apply HubSpot branding to the artifact you just created.

What the Skill Knows

Typography

The brand fonts are HubSpot Sans (body copy and headlines) and HubSpot Serif (headlines and subheads only — never body copy). When those fonts aren't available, Lexend Deca is the only approved fallback — not Playfair, Georgia, or any other serif. For email, use Arial.

  • Only three Lexend Deca weights are approved: Light (300), Medium (500), Bold (700)
  • In Google Slides, use Lexend Deca for everything — HubSpot Sans isn't available there
  • Body text: Light/300, line-height 1.35. Headlines: line-height 1.1
  • Sentence case for all headings and body copy — capitalize only the first word and proper nouns
  • Left or center alignment only — never right-aligned or justified

Color Palette

Primary colors:

HubSpot Orange
#FF4800
HubSpot Teal
#042729
HubSpot Cream
#F8F5EE
HubSpot Neutral
#141414

Secondary accent colors: Magenta (#46062B), Lavender (#D6C2D9), Pink (#FCC3DC), Purple (#F9F5FF), Sage (#B9CDBE)

Accessibility — Text Color Rules

  • Light backgrounds → Neutral text. Cream, Sage, Lavender, Pink, and Purple are all "light."
  • Dark backgrounds → Cream text. Teal, Magenta, Neutral, and Orange are "dark" — use Cream (#F8F5EE), not white.
  • Orange text on web: never. Orange text is not accessible in HTML artifacts. It's fine in slides/print, but not on the web, and it must never exceed ~25% of any composition.

Logo

  • Always the real SVG — never recreate the logo with text
  • Orange logo on all brand backgrounds (Cream, Teal, Sage, Lavender, Purple, Pink, Magenta, Neutral) — do not switch to white on dark/Teal backgrounds
  • Cream logo only on a full-flood Orange background (or over photography)
  • Keep clear space around the logo — minimum one "H" width on all sides

Google Slides Templates

The skill knows the full slide template system. Tell Claude what type of slide you need and it will apply the correct layout:

  • Cover: Full-bleed Teal, Cream title (Bold), orange period on the final word of the hero headline (once per deck only)
  • Agenda: Cream background, Orange numbered items
  • Section divider: Teal background, Cream heading
  • Body — text only, bullets, two-column, three-column, four-column
  • Stats/data: Large Bold numbers in Teal or Orange
  • Quote, timeline, comparison table, FAQ, closing/thank you

Every content slide includes a running header (HubSpot wordmark left, section title right) and a slide number at bottom right.

Key Rules to Know

  • The orange period at the end of a headline is used once per deck, on the cover/hero headline only — not on every slide title
  • One sub-palette per slide or card — don't mix multiple secondary colors in the same composition
  • Orange as a full flood is fine for slides and print, but not on the web
  • Never use Roboto, Inter, Calibri, Playfair, or Georgia — Lexend Deca is the only approved fallback
  • All blog image outputs (charts, tables, visualizations) are delivered as PNG files — not HTML, SVG, or JPEG
