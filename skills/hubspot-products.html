HubSpot Products

This skill is shared with all HubSpot blog writers — you should have access automatically. To activate it, go to Customize → Skills in Claude and look for it under Shared Skills. Select the skill, then toggle it on in the top right. If you don't see the skill, reach out to your manager.

Current version: v3

Overview

The HubSpot Products skill is the starting point for writers covering any aspect of HubSpot. It surfaces accurate, comprehensive information about HubSpot's products — what each hub does, what's included in each tier, how features are priced, and how products relate to one another — by searching and fetching live HubSpot pages rather than relying on training data or a stored snapshot. Pricing pages, product pages, and the official Product & Services Catalog are checked before any HubSpot fact goes into Claude's output.

Why It Exists: HubSpot renames products, changes prices, and moves features between tiers on a regular cadence. Claude's training data on this is reliably out of date, and stale facts are especially damaging when they end up in published content. This skill treats live pages as the only source of truth.

What It Looks Up

  • Pricing, tiers, seat costs, and contact limits — from hubspot.com/pricing/<hub>
  • Product capabilities and positioning — from hubspot.com/products/*
  • Feature-to-tier mapping — from the pricing comparison table for the relevant hub
  • Hard limits and specs — from the official Product & Services Catalog (legal.hubspot.com)

When It Activates

The skill is designed to trigger automatically — you don't always need to invoke it explicitly.

  • Direct questions about HubSpot pricing, tiers, seats, features, or capabilities
  • Content work — any brief, draft, outline, or post you're writing or editing that mentions a HubSpot product, price, tier, or feature, even if you never ask a HubSpot question directly

If a HubSpot fact is about to appear in Claude's output, this skill should catch it and verify against a live source first.

How to Use

Example 1: It triggers on its own

Write an intro paragraph for a post comparing HubSpot Marketing Hub tiers.

Example 2: Invoke it explicitly

What's the per-seat price for Sales Hub Professional?
Use the HubSpot Products skill.

Important Notes

Always live — no snapshot

  • There's no stored data to go stale. Every lookup goes to a live HubSpot page.
  • For feature-to-tier questions, Claude checks the hub's pricing comparison table, not the feature's marketing page — the marketing page won't tell you which tier it's in.
  • If a live page won't render, Claude will say so and flag which source it fell back to rather than presenting a secondary number as authoritative.

What This Prevents

  • Stale pricing: HubSpot has shifted to per-seat pricing and changed rates multiple times — training data gets this wrong
  • Wrong hub assignments: Features get moved between hubs; live comparison tables catch this
  • Hallucinated feature availability: Claude won't assert a feature exists in a tier it can't confirm on a live page
  • Silent errors in drafts: The skill applies even when you're just writing, not asking questions — so stale facts don't slip through undetected
