Brief QA

This skill is shared with all HubSpot blog writers — you should have access automatically. To activate it, go to Customize → Skills in Claude and look for it under Shared Skills. Select the skill, then toggle it on in the top right. If you don't see the skill, reach out to your manager.

Overview

Brief QA catches AI-generated hallucinations in HubSpot blog briefs before you start writing. It verifies internal and competitor URLs, checks product names against live HubSpot data, and flags suspicious pricing claims.

Why it matters: HubSpot briefs are AI-generated and may contain broken links or outdated product information. This skill catches errors upfront and prevents rework.

What It Checks

  • Internal URLs: Validates all HubSpot blog links actually exist using sitemap data
  • Competitor URLs: Verifies external links are accessible
  • Product Names: Cross-references against live HubSpot products page
  • Pricing Claims: Flags pricing information that needs manual verification
  • Content Freshness: Reports last-modified dates for referenced articles

When to Use

  • Immediately after receiving a new blog brief from the content team
  • Before starting research or outlining
  • When you suspect a brief might have outdated references
  • For any brief that cites HubSpot products or internal content

How to Use

  1. Copy your blog brief from the content team
  2. Paste it into Claude with: "QA this brief using the Brief QA skill"
  3. Claude will verify all URLs, products, and claims
  4. Review flagged issues before starting your outline

Tips & Best Practices

Do This

  • Run Brief QA before you start outlining or researching
  • Fix broken URLs immediately — don't wait until writing
  • Flag stale content (1+ years old) to your editor
  • Always verify pricing claims manually (Brief QA flags but doesn't verify)

Avoid This

  • Skipping QA because the brief "looks fine"
  • Ignoring stale content warnings (relevance matters)
  • Citing pricing without manual verification
  • Using Brief QA after you've already written the post
