Brief QA

Download Skill

Important: HubSpot briefs are AI-generated and may contain broken links or outdated product information. Always run the Brief QA skill before starting to write.

When to Use

Run Brief QA immediately after receiving any AI-generated brief — before you start outlining or writing. Even if the brief looks fine, hallucinated URLs are common and invisible until checked.

Overview

The Brief QA skill catches AI hallucinations in your content briefs by verifying internal and competitor URLs, confirming product names against live HubSpot pages, checking pricing claims, and flagging stale content. It's designed to run before you outline, not after you've written the post.

What It Checks

  • Internal URLs: Verifies links against the HubSpot sitemap — flags broken or redirected pages
  • Competitor URLs: Confirms competitor links resolve and point to the right content
  • Product Names: Cross-references product and feature names against live HubSpot pages
  • Pricing Claims: Flags any pricing data that may be outdated or unverifiable
  • Content Freshness: Checks last-modified dates and flags content older than 12 months

Example Output

The skill returns a structured QA report with status indicators:

  • ✅ Verified — URL resolves, content matches
  • ⚠️ Needs Review — page exists but content may have changed
  • ❌ Broken — URL returns 404 or redirect loop
  • 🔍 Manual Check Required — pricing or product claim needs human verification

Installation

  1. Download the skill: Click the button above to access the skill package
  2. Open Claude Settings: Go to claude.ai → Settings → Capabilities → Skills
  3. Install the skill: Drag and drop the Brief QA.zip into the Skills upload area
  4. Activate the skill: Enable "Brief QA" in your skills list

How to Use

  1. Copy your full AI-generated brief into a new Claude chat
  2. Tell Claude: "Run this brief through the Brief QA skill"
  3. Review the QA report and fix any flagged issues before outlining
  4. Update your brief with verified information

Tips & Best Practices

Do This

  • Run Brief QA before outlining, not after you've already written sections
  • Fix broken URLs immediately — don't flag and move on
  • Flag stale content (1+ year old) for your editor to review
  • Manually verify any pricing claims before publishing

Avoid This

  • Skipping Brief QA because "the brief looks fine" — hallucinations are invisible until checked
  • Ignoring stale content warnings — outdated stats get flagged for a reason
  • Citing any pricing data without manual verification from live product pages
  • Running the skill after you've already written the article — fix the brief first
