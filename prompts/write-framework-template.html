WRITE Framework Template

Overview

This is the blank WRITE Framework template — your starting point for every AI-assisted writing task. Copy the template below, fill in each section with your specific details, and paste it into Claude. The more specifically you fill it in, the better the output.

The Template

W -- WHO (Persona):
You are [role]. You have [background/expertise]. Your audience is [specific audience description].

R -- RESOURCES (Context):
- Topic: [article topic]
- Target keyword: "[keyword phrase]"
- Pain point: [what problem does the reader have?]
- Competitive landscape: [what does existing content look like?]
- HubSpot angle: [how does HubSpot relate to this topic?]
- Additional context: [any other relevant background]

I -- INSTRUCTIONS:
[Numbered list of specific steps Claude should follow]
1. [First thing to do]
2. [Second thing to do]
3. [Continue as needed]

T -- TERMS (Constraints):
- Format: [outline / draft / bullets / etc.]
- Length: [word count or range]
- Tone: [Bold Illuminator / conversational / formal / etc.]
- Structure: [any structural requirements]
- Rules: [things to avoid or include]

E -- EXPECTED OUTCOME:
[Describe exactly what the output should look like]
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [Specific deliverable 2]
- [Include an example if possible]

Quick Tips

  • Start with W and E: Define who Claude is and what you want before anything else
  • Be specific in Instructions: "Write 6–8 H2 sections" is better than "write an outline"
  • Always include an example: Show Claude what good looks like in the Expected Outcome section
  • Save successful prompts: When a prompt produces great output, save it for reuse
  • Iterate: Your first draft of a prompt won't be perfect — refine it over time
